# Conducting VC Fundraising & Dilution Modeling

## Goal
To orchestrate dynamic pitch assessments, build investor narratives (Sequoia/a16z models), simulate intensive VC partner Q&A, and perform seed-stage valuation/dilution (SAFE/Convertible Note) math.

## When to Use This Skill
- When outlining pitch deck skeletons and verbal/written investment thesis arguments.
- When organizing investor outreach CRM sequences and cohort calendars.
- When modeling dilution math for SAFEs (Pre-money vs. Post-money) and Convertible Notes.

### Do Not Use This Skill For:
- Formulating public securities offerings or standard corporate bank loan underwriting.

## Plan-Validate-Execute Workflow
1. **Plan (Narrative & Capital Needs):**
   - Identify total capital target, primary use of funds, planned runway length, and target investor profile (Angels, Micro-VCs, institutional VC).
2. **Validate (Qualitative & Quantitative Check):**
   - Run a Pitch Readiness Audit (confirming inclusion of Problem, Solution, Market Size/TAM, Traction, Business Model, Team, and the Ask).
   - Test SAFE math: Verify standard 20% discount rates or valuation cap constraints.
   - Run the Interactive QA simulator: Highlight answers to the 4 VC Risk Vectors (TAM/Market, Exit potential, Team, and Tech/Moat).
3. **Execute (Deliver Output Products):**
   - **Venture Pitch Narrative:** Generate standard Sequoia-style 10-slide outline structure.
   - **Outreach Playbook:** Draft week-by-week calendar CRM outreach sequences (leads, warm introductions, partner pitches, follow-ups).
   - **Cap Table Dilution Matrix:** Model the financial equity post-money impact of SAFEs on current founders' equity shares.

## Design Rules & Guidelines

### Pitch Narrative Principles
- Bullet points on slide outlines must focus on concrete traction metrics (e.g., "$50k MRR, 15% MoM growth") rather than generic claims ("rapid user adoption").
- Ensure a logical connection between the Problem, Solution, and TAM slide valuations.

### Dilution & Cap Table Math Rules
- Clearly distinguish between **Pre-money SAFEs** (dilute founders and investors together at the equity round) and **Post-money SAFEs** (dilute existing founders directly, locking in investor percentage).
- Proactively flag heavy founder dilution: If early SAFE rounds aggregate over 30% dilution before Series A, issue an expert WARNING advice block.

## Example Implementation Checklist
Copy this checklist during fundraising cycles:

- [ ] Run narrative audit on existing deck contents.
- [ ] Outline pitch slides based on Sequoia or YC templates.
- [ ] Simulate partner-level Q&A on core market/tech risks.
- [ ] Calculate post-money SAFE cap table equity allocations.
- [ ] Model conversion dilution from SAFE caps and discounts.
- [ ] Assemble week-by-week CRM outreach and email sequencing guides.
- [ ] Provide fully computed cap table allocation tables.
