# Contractor and Talent Management

## Goal
To manage recruiting pipelines, structure technical candidate screenings, and govern independent contractor workflows. Ensures compliant freelance contracting, high-quality onboarding, and legal-grade terminations without emotional bias.

## When to Use This Skill
- When designing technical screening guidelines and recruitment pipelines for new talent.
- When organizing interview questions and scorecards to assess technical hard skills.
- When drafting contractor agreements, Statements of Work (SOW), or freelancer templates.
- When managing dispute resolutions, contractor offboarding, or contract terminations.

### Do Not Use This Skill For:
- Full-time employee payroll tax structuring or multi-jurisdictional labor law consulting.

## The Talent Lifecycle Workflow

```text
 ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐
 │ Sourcing & Pipeline │ ───▻ │ Technical Screening │ ───▻ │ Contracting & SOW   │ ───▻ │ Offboarding & Term  │
 │  Define Profile (1) │      │  Ratio grading (2)  │      │ IP Transfers (3)     │      │ Unemotional Firm (4)│
 └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘
```

1. **Phase 1: Sourcing & Pipeline Management (Strategy):**
   - Define exact candidate profile parameters: stack experience, communication level, and compensation bandwidth.
   - Set up standard stages: Sourced, Screened, Technical Interview, Contractor SOW, and Onboarded.
2. **Phase 2: Technical Screening & Interviewing:**
   - Construct objective technical questions, screening guides, and practical coding exercises.
   - Use standardized, metric-driven grading matrices (scale 1-5 or A-F) to evaluate portfolios and verify claims without relying on generic resumes.
3. **Phase 3: Contracting & Statements of Work (Compliance):**
   - Gather contract terms: scope of work, deliverables, deadlines, and rates.
   - Ensure explicit contract hygiene markers: clear Parties, Term & Termination rules, Intellect Property transfer upon final payment, and explicit Independent Contractor Status.
4. **Phase 4: Offboarding, Disputes & Terminations:**
   - Issue concise, direct, polite termination emails firmly anchored on proven performance issues (e.g., missed milestones), entirely excluding emotional or conversational remarks.

## Design Rules & Guidelines

### Talent Evaluation & Screening Rules
- Design interview questions to check for actual problem-solving and implementation. Avoid trick logic.
- Ensure technical screening requirements are kept under a 90-minute limit to maintain high candidate satisfaction.

### Legal Hygiene Rules
- Agreements must include explicit definitions of Intellectual Property (IP) transfer upon final payment.
- Terms of termination must contain clear notice periods (e.g., immediate termination upon material breach).
- Guardrails check: Do not append arbitrary restrictive covenants (like high-risk non-solicitation or non-competes) unless explicitly instructed, keeping the draft clean and realistic.

### Professional Communication Tone
- All communications conveying negative news (rejections or terminations) must be concise, objective, polite, and firmly centered on factual terms. Completely omit emotional, defensive, or accusatory statements.

## Example Implementation Checklist
Copy this checklist during talent operations tasks:

- [ ] Define the target role profile, tech stack expectations, and candidate compensation.
- [ ] Construct an objective technical screening questionnaire and score rating system.
- [ ] Structure agreements with clear Parties, Scope of Work, Compensation, and IP allocation.
- [ ] Confirm no unnecessary or non-customary non-solicitation clauses are present.
- [ ] For rejections or terminations, identify precise objective performance factors (e.g., missed deadlines).
- [ ] Draft communications and legal templates utilizing a firm, non-conversational, compliant tone.
- [ ] Review documentation and templates for professional structural consistency throughout.
