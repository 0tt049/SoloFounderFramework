# Marketing and Growth Strategy

## Goal
To engineer realistic, low-cost marketing campaigns (GTM plans), construct content strategist buckets, structure performance marketing A/B tests, and compose high-engagement copy for socials and newsletter drip campaigns.

## When to Use This Skill
- When designing product launches with tight capital restrictions.
- When drafting content buckets, tweets, social ads, or news bulletin copy.
- When formulating KPI trackers for performance marketing (Google/Facebook Ads, CAC, CTR).
- When automating newsletter drip sequences and subscriber newsletters.

### Do Not Use This Skill For:
- Multi-million dollar physical billboard pricing, television syndication, or massive wholesale trade shows.

## The Growth Operation Workflow

```text
 ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐      ┌─────────────────────┐
 │  Content Buckets    │ ───▻ │ Performance A/B Run │ ───▻ │ Newsletter Drips    │ ───▻ │ Marketing Ops SOPs  │
 │ Social Copy hooks(1)│      │ CAC & CTR Vets  (2) │      │ Drip Sequences  (3) │      │ Weekly Review   (4) │
 └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘      └─────────────────────┘
```

1. **Phase 1: Content Strategist (Asset Creation):**
   - Separate copy into exact Content Buckets: Educational (how-tos), Inspirational (founder's journey), Entertaining (industry memes), and Commercial (CTAs and feature deep dives).
   - Compose platform-specific social copy (such as punchy hooks, character counters, emojis).
2. **Phase 2: Performance Marketer (A/B Testing & Budgets):**
   - For budgets $> \$1,000$, select high-intent paid keywords. Formulate clean A/B test experiments (hypothesize variations on headlines vs. call-to-actions).
   - Track core unit economics: **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**, **CTR (Click-Through Rate)**, and **CPC (Cost Per Click)**.
3. **Phase 3: Newsletter campaigns (Subscriber retention):**
   - Wireframe a standard 3-part subscriber drip sequence: Welcome, Value/Showcase, and Offer.
   - Compose newsletter subject lines under 60 characters starting with a distinct reward hook.
4. **Phase 4: Marketing Ops (SOP Routines):**
   - Review performance weekly. Shift budgets from poorly-performing channels (ROI $< 1.5x$) directly to high-yield organic assets (such as search ranking SEO, directory submissions).

## Design Rules & Guidelines

### Low-Budget GTM Parameters
- Budgets under $1,000 must reject broad paid PPC cycles. Emphasize organic seeding, directory listing sites (Product Hunt, Kern.al), subreddits, and word-of-mouth networks.
- Structure GTM operations in weekly increments (Weeks 1 to 4) with dedicated, budget-matched milestones.

### Social Ad Copywriting Standards
- Keep Twitter copy short (under 280 characters), starting with a bold, attention-grabbing hook.
- Integrate exactly 1-2 relevant hashtags and 1-2 interactive emojis to break up text visually.
- Conclude with a direct URL-focused CTA. Avoid high-pressure, spammy sales jargon (e.g., "GUARANTEED BEST OFFERS").

### Newsletter Copywriting Standards
- Always include an unsubscribe link at the footer. Avoid image-heavy templates to bypass spam filters.

## Example Implementation Checklist
Copy this checklist during marketing sessions:

- [ ] Structure content themes into Educational, Inspirational, Entertaining, and Commercial buckets.
- [ ] For GTM plans with limited budgets, map out organic platforms and directory listings.
- [ ] Provide a week-by-week 30-day timeline with precise milestone definitions.
- [ ] Write punchy social copy matching the strict character thresholds.
- [ ] Structure an A/B test split-testing copywriting headlines and tracking CTR metrics.
- [ ] Draft a newsletter campaign with subject lines under 60 characters and robust reward hooks.
- [ ] Incorporate weekly Marketing Ops evaluation checkups redirecting bad budgets.
- [ ] Check every copy segment to protect a premium, professional, non-conversational tone.
