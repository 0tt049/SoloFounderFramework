# Writing Job Descriptions

## Goal
To draft clear, objective, and high-converting job descriptions tailored for solo-founder hires, with embedded tools for inclusive language auditing, pay equity standards, and output constraints.

## When to Use This Skill
- When drafting contractor or freelance briefs.
- When organizing recruiting profiles for core SaaS technical/non-technical roles.
- When restructuring open requirements to avoid qualification inflation and demographic bias.

### Do Not Use This Skill For:
- Writing long-form standard corporate bylaws or union agreements.

## Plan-Validate-Execute Workflow
1. **Plan (Intake Scoping):**
   - Identify company name, role name, salary band/compensation structure, work location (remote/hybrid), reporting lines, and 3-5 core deliverables.
2. **Validate (Bias & Inflation Audit):**
   - Pre-scan keywords to remove gendered/clichéd language (e.g. replacing "ninja" or "rockstar" with "expert" or "professional").
   - Audit required years of experience to match realistic market expectations and prevent credential inflation.
   - Limit the list of non-negotiable requirements to 5-7 key items to maximize applicant pool diversity.
3. **Execute (Render Standard JD):**
   - Format the JD using the standardized template: About the Company, About the Role, Outcomes (1st year key achievements), Core Requirements, Nice-to-haves, Benefits, and EEO statement.
   - Include clear compensation bands to promote compliance and equity.
   - Append a brief "Inclusive Language Audit Summary" listing key rephrasings and structural optimizations applied to the final output.

## Design Rules & Guidelines

### Communication Constraints
- Tone must remain objective, outcome-oriented, and highly welcoming.
- No emotional or excessively hype-based terms ("family culture", "hustle-first", "ninja") are allowed.
- Always include a compensation / salary range. If omitted, suggest a competitive bracket benchmarked to regional market standards.

### Diversity & Equity Rules
- Keep strict mandatory requirements distinct from learned on-the-job preferences.
- Standard corporate JD template must include a clear, compliant Equal Employment Opportunity (EEO) statement.

## Example Implementation Checklist
Copy this checklist during job specification creation:

- [ ] Run role intake (Title, mode, compensation, key deliverables).
- [ ] Scan and filter planned text to eliminate gendered words and clichés.
- [ ] Prune requirements down to 5-7 core milestones.
- [ ] Write impact-focused action verbs for responsibilities.
- [ ] Append the Equal Opportunity compliance clause.
- [ ] Deliver the complete, formatted job description with an accompanying bias audit summary.
